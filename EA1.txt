	As an officer of the WSU Cybersecurity Group student organization I helped correspond with the Spokane Cyber Cup (SCC) leaders to gain admission to the event, and organized transportation for club members to the venue. SCC is a team-based Capture-The-Flag event where teams work to score points by solving challenges. These challenges cover a wide variety of security topics including networking, encryption, open source intelligence, radio, large language models, and even a physical challenge in lockpicking. 
	During this competition, I chose to focus on finishing the easier questions first before moving onto harder ones. Topics I worked on included exploiting a large language model with improperly configured permissions, breaking ciphers, and SQL injection. I’m proud of my performance and my team scored well, but I do feel like I missed an opportunity for leadership. I scored the most points of my team by far, as many of my teammates were stuck on harder questions for much of the event’s duration. If I had delegated these easier questions, we could all have spent more time on the complex ones and less experienced team members could learn more from easy questions rather than getting stuck and frustrated on hard ones.
